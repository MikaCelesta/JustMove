The problem

Travel planning still asks users to do the hard parts manually.

Fragmented search

Flights, hotels, transport, policies, and travel documents are scattered across dozens of sources.

Generic ranking

Most platforms show the same filters to a business traveler, student, family, and vacation planner.

AI trust risk

Unverified chatbot answers can be outdated, expensive, or unsafe when travel details change quickly.

Product overview

From advice to action, without giving up control.

JustMove.AI searches verified travel sources, compares tradeoffs, builds personalized itineraries, and prepares bookings. It continues monitoring the journey for delays, cancellations, price changes, and better substitutions.

PersonalizedRanks options by traveler type, purpose, budget, timing, and comfort.
VerifiedGrounds key claims in APIs, official sources, and timestamped records.
ControlledRequires confirmation before spending money or exceeding policy limits.

Traveler-type personalization

Different travelers need different answers.

Business

Prioritizes punctual arrival, nearby hotels, fewer delays, and company policy compliance.

Vacation

Balances affordability, attractions, comfort, and public transportation access.

Budget

Surfaces lower-cost substitutions while explaining schedule and flexibility tradeoffs.

Family

Looks for larger rooms, family-friendly amenities, and simpler transfers.

AI ethics and safety

Trust is the product feature.

Travel decisions involve money, identity documents, strict schedules, and real-world consequences. JustMove.AI is designed to say what it knows, cite what it checked, and ask before acting.

Grounded recommendationsPrices, policies, availability, and visa risks come from live or official sources whenever possible.
Uncertainty handlingWhen data is unclear, the system flags uncertainty instead of pretending.
Privacy controlsCollects only needed trip data and protects sensitive information with strict access and retention rules.
Transparent incentivesSponsored or partner results should be labeled separately from best-fit recommendations.
